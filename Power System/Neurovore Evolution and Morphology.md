## Neurovore Evolution and Morphology

Neurovores evolve by **absorbing neural patterns** from the brains they consume. Each brain contains not only knowledge but also memories, symbolic associations, and emotional obsessions. These cognitive structures reshape the Neurovore’s internal CAS architecture, altering both **appearance and abilities**. As a result, Neurovores rarely share identical forms; their morphology reflects the **information they have absorbed**.

---

# Evolution Stages

### Stage 1 — **Larval Neurovore (N1)**

Newly emerged organisms with minimal cognitive structure.

Characteristics:

- small or amorphous bodies
    
- basic predatory instincts
    
- limited CAS activity
    

They primarily consume **animals or low-complexity neural tissue** to accumulate foundational cognition.

Appearance is unstable and often shifts rapidly.

---

### Stage 2 — **Adaptive Neurovore (N2)**

After consuming multiple brains, the Neurovore begins forming a stable neural architecture.

Characteristics:

- defined biological structure
    
- specialized predatory adaptations
    
- primitive CAS abilities
    

At this stage, **diet strongly influences form**.

Examples:

- consuming birds may lead to winged morphologies
    
- consuming aquatic animals may produce fluid-based bodies
    
- consuming predators may result in aggressive anatomical structures
    

---

### Stage 3 — **Cognitive Neurovore (N3)**

These individuals begin incorporating **human knowledge patterns**.

Characteristics:

- complex CAS abilities
    
- semi-stable identity structures
    
- problem-solving capability
    

Their forms often resemble **conceptual images extracted from human imagination**.

For instance:

- a victim obsessed with dragons may result in a large reptilian Neurovore
    
- a victim fascinated by machines may produce biomechanical structures
    

---

### Stage 4 — **Dominant Neurovore (N4)**

At this level, the Neurovore’s brain contains a dense network of absorbed cognition.

Characteristics:

- large-scale CAS manipulation
    
- stable and consistent physical form
    
- strategic hunting behavior
    

Their abilities reflect the **dominant cognitive themes** they have consumed.

Examples:

- physics researchers → energy manipulation abilities
    
- architects → structural or geometric manipulation
    
- martial artists → extreme kinetic control
    

---

### Stage 5 — **Hollow Neurovore (N5)**

Hollows represent the highest known stage of Neurovore evolution.

They possess extremely dense cognitive structures and powerful CAS capabilities.

Characteristics:

- unique and stable forms
    
- advanced intelligence
    
- large-scale actuation phenomena
    

Their appearance often becomes **symbolic rather than biological**, reflecting the strongest obsessions or knowledge clusters within their absorbed minds.

Examples include:

- **Dragon Hollows**  
    Created from victims with intense fascination for dragons. These Neurovores develop massive reptilian bodies and destructive thermal CAS abilities.
    
- **Phantom Hollows**  
    Formed from individuals obsessed with concepts like invisibility, ghosts, or intangibility. These Neurovores may possess partial phasing abilities.
    
- **Mythic Hollows**  
    When multiple victims share similar imaginative themes (fantasy creatures, heroes, monsters), the Neurovore may manifest an archetypal form drawn from collective imagery.
    
- **Concept Hollows**  
    Extremely rare entities whose abilities originate from abstract knowledge systems.  
    Example: consuming individuals obsessed with spatial theory could create a Hollow capable of **limited spatial traversal or dimensional distortion**.
    

---

# Key Variables Influencing Neurovore Form

Neurovore development is influenced by several factors:

**1. Cognitive Density**  
The intelligence and knowledge depth of consumed brains.

**2. Emotional Intensity**  
Strong obsessions or passions leave powerful imprints.

**3. Conceptual Consistency**  
Repeated exposure to similar ideas stabilizes certain forms.

**4. Knowledge Domain**  
Scientific or technical knowledge can produce highly dangerous CAS abilities.

---

# Resulting Diversity

Because each Neurovore evolves through different cognitive inputs, the species displays **extreme morphological diversity**. Some resemble animals, others mythical creatures, and some become abstract entities that barely resemble biological life.

For this reason, researchers often describe Neurovores as:

**“Predators of cognition that sculpt themselves from the minds they consume.”**
