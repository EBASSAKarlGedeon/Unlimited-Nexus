# Government Classification System for Cognitive-Actuation Users

International regulatory bodies classify individuals under the **Cognitive Capability Index (CCI)**, which measures two variables:

- **K-Level (Knowledge Depth)** – how well a person understands a phenomenon
    
- **E-Level (Execution Capacity)** – how strongly they can manifest it
    

An individual's official rating appears as **Kx–Ey**.

Example: **K4–E2** means high theoretical understanding but low operational ability.

---

## Class 0 — Non-Actuators

**Designation:** C0

These individuals possess no measurable cognitive-actuation capability.

They may study the phenomenon academically but cannot produce effects.

Most of the population belongs to this class.

---

## Class 1 — Latent Cognitives

**Designation:** C1

Individuals with measurable **omniscience patterns** but no reliable execution.

Typical profiles:

- scientists
    
- engineers
    
- students trained in theoretical models
    

They understand phenomena deeply but cannot trigger them physically.

Example: a physicist who can model plasma formation but cannot generate it.

---

## Class 2 — Limited Actuators

**Designation:** C2

Individuals capable of **small-scale phenomena** with stable control.

Typical capabilities include:

- localized heat generation
    
- small electrostatic discharge
    
- minor pressure manipulation
    

These users are commonly employed in **industrial and research environments**.

---

## Class 3 — Operational Actuators

**Designation:** C3

Users capable of **consistent and practical effects**.

Governments regulate this group heavily because their abilities can have significant real-world consequences.

Examples:

- controlled plasma arcs
    
- fluid projection
    
- kinetic impulse bursts
    

Many work in **energy production, disaster response, or military programs**.

---

## Class 4 — Strategic Actuators

**Designation:** C4

Rare individuals capable of **large-scale or highly complex phenomena**.

Their abilities may influence infrastructure, environment, or military balance.

Examples:

- large thermal events
    
- electromagnetic disruption
    
- structural manipulation of matter
    

These individuals are usually monitored or recruited by national governments.

---

## Class 5 — Singularities

**Designation:** C5

Extremely rare cases where knowledge and execution approach theoretical limits.

Characteristics include:

- near-perfect translation of knowledge into action
    
- ability to manipulate highly complex systems
    
- extremely efficient neural coherence
    

Because of their potential impact, most nations treat C5 individuals as **strategic assets equivalent to weapons of mass influence**.

---

# Additional Government Metrics

### Cognitive Stability Rating (CSR)

Measures psychological stability while performing phenomena.

Unstable users risk **uncontrolled outputs**.

### Knowledge Breadth Index (KBI)

Measures how many scientific domains a user can operationalize.

Example:

- thermodynamics
    
- electromagnetism
    
- fluid dynamics
    

### Actuation Efficiency (AE)

Measures energy cost vs output.

Highly efficient users can produce stronger effects with less neural strain.
